National Heroes of Pakistan

Quaid e Azam

Mohammad Ali Jinnah

Muhammad Ali Jinnah, widely revered as Quaid-e-Azam (Great Leader), is the founding father of Pakistan and one of the most influential figures in the history of the Indian subcontinent. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi, Jinnah was a brilliant lawyer, politician, and statesman.

Allama Mohammad Iqbal

Allama Muhammad Iqbal, often referred to as the "Spiritual Father of Pakistan," was a renowned philosopher, poet, and politician whose ideas and vision greatly influenced the creation of Pakistan. Born on November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, Iqbal was a gifted thinker who combined his deep knowledge of Western philosophy with his profound understanding of Islamic teachings.

Liaquat Ali Khan

Liaquat Ali Khan was a key figure in the struggle for Pakistan's independence and served as the country's first Prime Minister. Born on October 1, 1895, in Karnal, India, Liaquat was a close associate of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah and played a crucial role in the Pakistan Movement.
